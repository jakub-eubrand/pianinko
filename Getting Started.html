Getting Started in 3 Steps
1

Open the App on Your Computer

Find Little Maestro App.html in this folder. Double-click it — it opens directly in your web browser. Chrome or Edge recommended. No installation needed. Do not try to open it on a phone or tablet — it requires a computer keyboard or MIDI keyboard to function correctly.

2

Connect a Piano or Keyboard (Optional but Recommended)

Connect a real piano or MIDI keyboard via USB before opening the app — it will detect it automatically. You can also play using your computer keyboard or the on-screen keys. A 25-key or 49-key USB MIDI keyboard is plenty for all beginner content.

3

Pick a World and Start Playing

Click any song to start. Use Turtle speed for beginners, Normal for practice, and work up to Rocket speed as skills improve. Earn stars on each song to unlock the next world!

Tips for Parents & Teachers
Start slow, literally. Use Turtle speed (🐢) for all new songs. The goal is accuracy first — speed follows naturally. Most kids want to rush to Rocket 🚀 immediately — encourage them to earn 3 stars at Turtle speed before moving up.
Short sessions beat long ones. 10–15 minutes daily is more effective than an hour on weekends. The app is designed to make short sessions feel satisfying through the star and world progression.
For real piano students, Worlds 1, 2, and 3 (Primer Basics, Primer Two Hands, Primer Skills) align with standard beginner method book content. Start there before moving into the main song progression.
MIDI keyboard tip: Any USB MIDI keyboard works. A 25-key or 49-key keyboard is plenty for all beginner content. Full 88-key is not required — even a $40 mini keyboard gives the full experience.
Progress is saved in the browser. Always open the app in the same browser on the same computer. If you clear your browser history or cache, progress will reset. To back up progress, avoid clearing browser data on the computer you use for the app.
Want to add your own songs? The included Customization Guide.md explains exactly how to add songs, rename worlds, and adjust difficulty — no programming experience required.
